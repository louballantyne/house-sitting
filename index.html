Looking after the parrots

There are 5 Green-Cheeked conures and 2 African Greys.

Green-Cheeked Conures:

  • Rio (cage on his own)
  • Rolo and Toto (next to Rio) – Toto has yellow on his belly
  • Caesar and Pixie – Pixie has yellow on her belly

African Greys:

  • Echo (biggest cage)
  • Smokey

Feeding Schedule:

  • They all have an unlimited amount of pellets in their pens. If these run out, top them up.
    • Rio tends to make his a bit gross by mushing up fresh food in there. If it gets gross, remove and discard it.
    • The conures eat Harrisons Lifetime Fine pellets.
    • Smokey eats Harrisons High Potency Coarse.
    • Echo is a fusspot and currently eats Harrisons High Potency Fine made into a mush with hot water. Also offer Lafeber Gourmet pellets.
  • Morning routine:
    • Remove seed bowls from the greys.
    • Remove and wash Echo’s mushy pellet bowl.
    • Wash all food and water bowls.
    • Replace water and change newspaper.
  • While bowls are drying, offer pre-breakfast:
    • Little piece of toast for everyone.
    • Echo may prefer scrambled egg or toast.
    • Fresh fruit and vegetables (washed in a sieve):
      • 7 sugar snap peas
      • 3 slices of carrot
      • About half a tin of sweetcorn
      • 2 baby corns
      • 2 big slices of pepper
      • A thick slice of beetroot
      • Broccoli
      • Pomegranate seeds (4 each for conures, 8 each for greys)
      • 6 grapes (half each for small birds)
      • Optional: apple or other fruit
  • This is a rough guide! They can have more or different amounts.
  • Evening feeding (~5pm):
    • Conures get a Nutriberry.
    • Greys get seeds:
      • Echo: very small amount from two seed bags.
      • Smokey: larger amount from the bag closest to the door (labelled).
  • Evening routine:
    • Shut dark blue blinds on French doors and between dining room and kitchen (between 6–8pm).

Bribery

All birds can be bribed! Use what helps. Tips:

  • Caesar: bribed with toast or nutriberries (give Pixie some too).
  • Smokey: bribed with toast.
  • Echo: loves scrambled egg (kept in fridge, can be microwaved in glass jug).
  • To get birds back into their pen, show their favorite food going into it.
  • Rolo and Toto: best lured back with safflower seeds, toast, or nutriberries.
  • Caesar, Pixie, and Rio: bribed with all of the above.
  • Important: Do not let conures from different cages out at the same time—they will fight!

Individual Bird Notes

Caesar

10 years old. Very confident and cage-defensive. Sneaky with food doors. Nippy. Will fight with Toto and Rio. Obsessed with nesting (blankets).

Pixie

5 years old. Flighty but sweet. May fly onto you. Breathing may sound funny after flying due to old nostril damage. Will fight with Toto and Rio.

Rolo

8 years old. Not hand tame but may sit on your head or hand for seeds. Follows Toto.

Toto

7 years old. Confident. May fly to your shoulder. Escape artist—sneaks through food doors. Nest-obsessed. Will fight with Caesar, Pixie, and Rio.

Echo

10 years old. Plucks/barbers feathers. Sweet but spunky. Keep off curtain rail, cooker hood, and kitchen door.

Smokey

Over 40 years old. Hilarious but cage-defensive and unpredictable. Best kept in cage. Toast works for bribery.
